# Saltwater Squish Ecommerce Plan

## Direction

Use **Saltwater Squish** as the store name. Keep **Salt + Sun + Squish** as the campaign line.

Positioning: coastal sensory squishies organized by feel, texture, and collectability.

Voice: sunny, tactile, slightly childlike, and clear enough for gift buyers.

Core copy:

- Hero: "Squishies for the salt-and-sun set."
- Offer: "Free Keyboard Clicker fidget with every $20+ order."
- Product promise: "Pocket-sized coastal fidgets to squeeze, collect, and gift."

## Site IA

- Announcement bar: free clicker offer
- Header: Shop All, Shop by Feel, Bundles, Drop Films, Cart
- Hero: brand signal, product mood, primary shop CTA
- Shop by Feel: Ocean + Coastal, Slow-Rise Bakery, Clear Jelly + Icy, Crunchy + Slushy
- Product grid: texture filters, search, add to cart
- Bundle section: cart progress toward the $20 free gift
- Drop films: five 30-second launch video concepts
- Footer: shipping, returns, safety, Instagram

## Product Taxonomy

Primary collections:

- New Drops
- Shop All
- Ocean + Coastal
- Slow-Rise Bakery
- Clear Jelly + Icy
- Crunchy + Slushy
- Cloud-Soft Squish
- Bundles + Gifts
- Under $10

Filters:

- Feel: slow-rise, crunchy, slushy, clear jelly, cloud-soft, icy
- Shape: ocean, food, fruit, ball, bar, mini
- Color: pink, blue, peach, clear, pastel
- Price: under $10, $10-$15, bundles
- Giftable: party favors, desk fidgets, stocking stuffers

## Product Naming System

Use collectible names built from a coastal cue plus a sensory cue plus a shape.

Launch names in the prototype:

- Sea Glass Slush Ball
- Shell Shop Soap Bar
- Laguna Peach Ice
- Malibu Moon Bun
- Pearl Pop Bead Ball
- Coconut Cove Cloud

Avoid supplier-facing material terms like "vaseline squishy" in customer copy. Use "clear gloss", "jelly-soft", "oil-slick soft", or "cloud-soft" instead.

## Interaction Design

The bubble reference is adapted as an ocean foam layer, not copied directly.

- Ambient bubbles drift behind the shop and lightly repel from the cursor.
- Clicking empty beach/water space spawns a soft foam splash.
- Add-to-cart sends a tiny product bubble toward the cart.
- Product cards remain normal, stable rectangles for scanning and shopping.
- Filters and collection links are semantic controls with obvious selected states.
- Reduced-motion users get static bubbles and no pop/fly animations.

## Splash Canvas Adaptation

The Google Arts & Culture Splash Canvas reference is adapted as **Squish Splash Studio**.

- The full-screen art-stage becomes a shoppable page section, so commerce remains visible.
- Sea-creature brush behavior becomes product-texture brush behavior: Sea Glass, Shell Gloss, Pearl Pop, Tide Smudge, and Sand Scrape.
- GPU liquid simulation is approximated with lightweight canvas blooms, blur, bead dots, smudge strokes, and clear strokes.
- The brush tray uses real buttons with selected states, so the playful layer does not hide essential controls.
- The ordinary cursor is replaced on the canvas by the selected brush creature, giving the Art & Culture-style "character as brush" feeling.
- A random splash button gives keyboard and non-drag users access to the experience.
- The studio CTA sends shoppers back to the product grid.

## Shopify Handoff

Must-have setup:

- Shopify catalog with launch SKUs, prices, tags, collection assignments, and actual product photos
- Link payment enabled
- Automatic free Keyboard Clicker gift rule at $20+
- Cart progress message: "You're $X away from a free Keyboard Clicker"
- Shipping, returns, safety, contact, and privacy pages
- Email capture and abandoned cart flow
- Instagram and ad pixels configured before campaigns

Recommended product fields:

- Title
- Subtitle or short description
- Price
- Product type / collection
- Feel tag
- Size
- Safety or age note
- Inventory
- Shipping weight
- Media: 1 hero photo, 3 detail photos, 1 short video
